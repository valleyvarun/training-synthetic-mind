1. Title:

2. Aim:
  • The aim of this research is to explore and prototype a sketch-based interface for CAD that allows designers to interact with CAD software through drawing.
  • By integrating AI and multimodal datasets, the project investigates how such a system could interpret hand-drawn sketches into structured CAD geometry and CAD instructions, enabling more intuitive, creative, and flexible workflows.
  • It also examines how these datasets could be inclusively created, owned, and shared‚Äîwith contributor compensation‚Äîto support collaborative, human-centered AI development in design.

11. Rhetorical Argument
If design is an argument, this project argues that future AI systems must be trained on transparent, intentional, and ethically sourced datasets, and that creators of digital assets should be recognized and compensated for their contributions. It challenges the current model of scraped data pipelines and proposes a participatory, provenance-aware, multimodal alternative.
12. Capstone Project Outline

My current project explores a sketch-based interface for CAD software, enabling designers to draw freely and have their sketches interpreted into structured CAD geometry and commands. This system is intended to make digital design workflows more intuitive, flexible, and aligned with traditional creative processes.

The prototype will integrate artificial intelligence trained on multimodal datasets‚Äîcombining sketches, CAD files, annotations, and models‚Äîto understand and translate hand-drawn inputs. The aim is to support diverse design fields such as architecture, urban planning, and product design by bridging the gap between informal ideation and formal digital modeling.

The project also investigates ethical dataset creation and digital asset ownership. By incorporating secure, transparent systems for attribution and compensation, it proposes a framework where contributors of training data can retain rights and receive value for their work.
