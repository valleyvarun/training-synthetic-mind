11. Rhetorical Argument
If design is an argument, this project argues that future AI systems must be trained on transparent, intentional, and ethically sourced datasets, and that creators of digital assets should be recognized and compensated for their contributions. It challenges the current model of scraped data pipelines and proposes a participatory, provenance-aware, multimodal alternative.
12. Capstone Project Outline

My final project will be a prototype for a multimodal dataset creation platform designed to support the next generation of AI training systems. The platform will allow users to upload complex digital assets—such as 3D models, videos, audio, images, and text—that can be combined into richly structured, high-quality multimodal datasets.

What sets this platform apart is its integration of NFTs to establish ownership, attribution, and provenance for each contributed asset. Contributors will not only retain authorship over their work, but will also earn shares or tokens in the platform based on the value, usage, and volume of their contributions.

By enabling transparent, incentive-driven data creation, the project aims to offer an alternative to the opaque and extractive data pipelines that dominate current AI development. The prototype will explore how simulation platforms like NVIDIA Omniverse can be leveraged to generate high-fidelity synthetic data and how decentralized infrastructures can underpin equitable participation in the AI data economy.

Note: This project will be limited to the creation of the platform and will not attempt to build or train an AI model, due to technical and computational limitations. Creating such a model would require large-scale infrastructure and resources currently only accessible to major AI companies.
